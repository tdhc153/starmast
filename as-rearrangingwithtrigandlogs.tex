\subsection*{Q1}\label{q1}}
\addcontentsline{toc}{subsection}{Q1}

Solve the trigonometric equations in radians. Answers are given in exact
for

1.1 For \(sin(x)=\frac{\sqrt2}{2}\), x is equal to \(\frac{\pi}{2}\) or
0.785. 1.2 For \(cos(2x+1)=\frac{1}{2}\), x is equal to
\(\frac{\pi-3}{6}\) or 0.0234. 1.3 For \(tan(5x-1)=\frac{\sqrt2}{2}\), x
is equal to 0.323. 1.4 For \(cos(x^2+4x+3)=1\), x is equal to -1 or -3.
To do this, you use that \(cos^{-1}(1)=0\) and so you need to solve the
quadratic equation \(x^2+4x+3=0\).
